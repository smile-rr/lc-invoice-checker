# UCP 600 & ISBP 821 — Invoice Checking Rules Reference

> This document captures all UCP 600 articles and ISBP 821 sections relevant to **commercial invoice examination** in a Letter of Credit (LC) documentary presentation.

---

## Part 1: UCP 600 Articles — Invoice Checking

| Article | Title | Rule Summary | Invoice Implication | Discrepancy Example |
|---------|-------|-------------|---------------------|---------------------|
| **Art. 2** | Definitions | Defines key terms: "complying presentation", "document", "honour", "negotiation", "presenter" | A presentation is complying only if it conforms to the terms and conditions of the LC, UCP 600 provisions, and international standard banking practice | Invoice must satisfy ALL LC terms to be considered complying |
| **Art. 4** | Credits vs. Contracts | Banks deal in documents only, not goods, services, or performance | Bank examines the invoice face value — it does not verify actual goods shipped | Invoice can be rejected on documentary grounds even if goods are correct |
| **Art. 5** | Documents vs. Goods/Services | Banks do not concern themselves with goods or underlying contracts | Invoice content is examined purely on its face against LC terms | An invoice with wrong description is discrepant regardless of actual shipment |
| **Art. 14a** | Standard for Examination | A nominated bank, confirming bank, and issuing bank must each determine if documents constitute a complying presentation | Invoice must comply on its face with the LC terms; strict compliance standard applies | Any deviation from LC field values = potential discrepancy |
| **Art. 14b** | Examination Period | Bank has a maximum of **5 banking days** following the day of presentation to determine compliance | Invoice must be examined and decision communicated within 5 banking days | Bank cannot reject invoice after 5-day window (precluded from claiming discrepancy) |
| **Art. 14c** | Presentation Period | Documents must be presented within **21 calendar days** after shipment date but not later than the LC expiry date | Invoice date and presentation date must both fall within the 21-day window from bill of lading date | Invoice presented on day 22 after B/L date = late presentation discrepancy |
| **Art. 14d** | Non-Contradiction Rule | Data in a document, when read in context with the LC and other documents, need not be identical but must not contradict data in the LC or any other stipulated document | Invoice data (amount, goods, ports) must not contradict Bill of Lading, Packing List, or LC terms | Invoice shows "500 units" but Packing List shows "1000 units" = contradiction |
| **Art. 14e** | Address Data | Addresses of beneficiary and applicant need not be the same as in the LC, but must be in the same country | Applicant and beneficiary addresses on invoice must be in the correct countries as per LC | Invoice shows buyer's address in wrong country = discrepancy |
| **Art. 14f** | Issuer of Documents | Documents not required by the LC must not be examined; if submitted, they may be returned | Invoice is always required; any additional unsolicited document attached does not affect LC compliance | Bank ignores extra attachment; only examines documents stipulated in LC |
| **Art. 14h** | Document Dating | A document may be dated prior to the issuance date of the LC but not later than the date of its presentation | Invoice date must not be later than the date it is presented to the bank | Invoice dated tomorrow but presented today = discrepancy (Art. 14h / 14i) |
| **Art. 14i** | Invoice Date | Invoice must not be dated later than the date of presentation | Invoice date ≤ Presentation date | Invoice dated 25 Apr but presented on 23 Apr = discrepant |
| **Art. 14j** | Transport Document Consistency | Ports of loading and discharge on transport docs must match LC; invoice may also state port info | If LC or invoice mentions port of loading/discharge, it must match Bill of Lading | Invoice shows "Port Klang" but B/L shows "Penang" = contradiction |
| **Art. 18a** | Invoice — Issuer & Appearance | Invoice must appear to be issued by the beneficiary; must be made out in the name of the applicant; need not be signed | Invoice must show **beneficiary as seller** and **applicant as buyer** | Invoice issued by a third party (not the beneficiary) = discrepant |
| **Art. 18b** | Invoice — Currency & Amount | Invoice must be made out in the same currency as the LC; invoice amount must not exceed the LC amount | Currency must match LC Field 32B; invoice total ≤ LC amount | Invoice for EUR 50,000 when LC is USD 50,000 = currency discrepancy |
| **Art. 18c** | Invoice — Goods Description | Description of goods, services, or performance on invoice must correspond with that appearing in the LC | Invoice goods description must **mirror** LC Field 45A exactly | "Industrial Widgets IW-2024" on invoice vs "Industrial Widgets Model No. IW-2024 CIF Singapore" in LC = discrepancy |
| **Art. 18d** | Invoice — No Requirement to Sign | Unlike other documents, commercial invoices are not required to be signed unless the LC explicitly states so | If LC does not require signature, unsigned invoice is acceptable | Bank cannot reject invoice solely for absence of signature unless LC stipulates it |
| **Art. 28f** | Insurance — Amount | Insurance coverage must be at least 110% of CIF/CIP value | If invoice is CIF, ensure insurance amount ≥ 110% of invoice value | Invoice CIF USD 50,000 but insurance only USD 50,000 (should be ≥ USD 55,000) = discrepancy |
| **Art. 30a** | Quantity Tolerance — "About" | Words "about" or "approximately" allow a 10% tolerance in amount, quantity, or unit price | If LC uses "about 1000 units", invoice may show 900–1100 units | LC says "about USD 50,000"; invoice for USD 54,000 = within tolerance, compliant |
| **Art. 30b** | Quantity Tolerance — No Keyword | 5% more or less in quantity allowed if LC does not state quantity in specific packaging units | If goods are in bulk (not unit-priced), ±5% quantity variance is acceptable | LC: 1000 MT of rice; invoice: 985 MT = within 5% tolerance, compliant |
| **Art. 30c** | Drawing Tolerance | A drawing under a LC may be for less than the LC amount, even if partial shipments are not permitted | Invoice amount may be less than LC amount | Invoice for USD 40,000 against LC of USD 50,000 = acceptable (underdrawing) |

## Part 2: ISBP 821 Sections Clarifying UCP 600 Invoice Articles

| ISBP 821 Section | Title | Clarifies UCP Article(s) | Detailed Guidance | Practical Rule for Invoice Checking |
|------------------|-------|--------------------------|-------------------|--------------------------------------|
| **Para. A1–A9** | General Principles | Art. 2, 14a | Defines "international standard banking practice"; examiner should consider whether documents comply based on their data content | Do not apply overly strict typographic standards; minor typos that do not create ambiguity are not discrepancies |
| **Para. A14** | Abbreviations | Art. 14a, 14d | Commonly understood abbreviations are acceptable (e.g., "Co." for "Company", "Ltd" for "Limited", "St" for "Street") | Invoice using "Co." instead of "Company" is NOT a discrepancy |
| **Para. A15** | Spelling Errors | Art. 14a | A misspelling or typing error that does not affect the meaning of a word or the document's compliance is not a discrepancy | "Indusrial" instead of "Industrial" — not discrepant if context is clear |
| **Para. A19** | Dates | Art. 14h, 14i | Date formats are acceptable in any order (DD MM YYYY, MM DD YYYY, etc.) as long as the date is unambiguous; non-calendar dates (e.g., "2nd quarter 2024") must be converted | Invoice dated "23/04/2026" and "April 23, 2026" are both acceptable formats |
| **Para. A24** | Originals vs Copies | Art. 17 | Unless the LC requires originals, a document produced by reprographic, automated, or computerised systems may be treated as original | Laser-printed invoice is acceptable as original unless LC specifically demands wet-ink original |
| **Para. B1–B5** | General Document Requirements | Art. 14a, 14d | Documents must be consistent with each other; the same entity may appear with slightly different names if it is clear they refer to the same party | "ABC Trading Pte Ltd" vs "ABC Trading" — acceptable if context confirms same entity |
| **Para. C1** | LC Number & Date on Invoice | Art. 18a | If the LC requires the invoice to bear the LC number, failure to state it is a discrepancy; if not required, the invoice may optionally state the LC number | Always check LC Field 46A — if it says "Invoice must reference LC No.", its absence = discrepancy |
| **Para. C2** | Signing of Invoice | Art. 18a, 18d | Invoice need not be signed unless the LC explicitly requires a signature; if required, any form of authentication (stamp, electronic signature) is acceptable | If LC Field 46A states "Signed Commercial Invoice", an unsigned invoice = discrepancy |
| **Para. C3** | Goods Description on Invoice | Art. 18c | The description of goods on the invoice must correspond with the LC description; it may be **more specific** but must **not be more general**; the description need not be identical word-for-word but must clearly correspond | LC: "1000 Units Industrial Widgets Model IW-2024 CIF Singapore"; Invoice: "500 Units Industrial Widgets" = discrepant (less specific in quantity AND general description) |
| **Para. C4** | Unit Price on Invoice | Art. 18b, 18c | If the LC stipulates a unit price, the invoice must reflect that unit price; a higher total arising from a higher quantity is still discrepant if unit price differs | LC: USD 50/unit × 1000 = USD 50,000; Invoice: USD 55/unit × 1000 = USD 55,000 = discrepant (unit price differs) |
| **Para. C5** | Applicant Name & Address on Invoice | Art. 18a | The applicant's name and address on the invoice must correspond with those in the LC (Field 50); minor formatting differences in address are acceptable if they do not create ambiguity | "123 Orchard Road Singapore" vs "123 Orchard Rd, Singapore 238888" — acceptable if same entity is clear; but wrong country = discrepant |
| **Para. C6** | Beneficiary Name & Address on Invoice | Art. 18a | The beneficiary's name on the invoice must correspond with the LC (Field 59); address need not be identical but must be in the same country | Different post code for beneficiary = NOT discrepant; different country = discrepant |
| **Para. C7** | Country of Origin | Art. 18c | If the LC requires country of origin to be stated on the invoice, absence is a discrepancy | LC Field 46A: "Invoice must state country of origin: Germany"; invoice with no origin stated = discrepant |
| **Para. C8** | Trade Terms (Incoterms) | Art. 18b, 18c | If the LC states a trade term (CIF, FOB, CFR, etc.), the invoice must reflect the same term and destination | LC: "CIF Singapore"; Invoice: "FOB Hamburg" = discrepant trade term |
| **Para. C9** | Multiple Invoices | Art. 14d, 18b | If multiple invoices are presented, their combined total must not exceed the LC amount; each invoice must individually comply | 3 invoices totalling USD 52,000 against LC of USD 50,000 = amount discrepancy |
| **Para. C10** | Charges & Deductions on Invoice | Art. 18b | Freight, insurance, or other charges on invoice must be consistent with the trade term; charges should not inflate total above LC amount | Invoice: Goods USD 48,000 + freight USD 5,000 = USD 53,000 against LC USD 50,000 = discrepant if CIF basis |
| **Para. D1–D10** | Transport Documents — Consistency with Invoice | Art. 14d | Ports, dates, vessel names, and goods description on transport documents must not contradict the invoice | Invoice: "Port Klang to Singapore"; B/L: "Penang to Singapore" = contradiction = discrepancy |
| **Para. E1–E10** | Insurance Documents | Art. 28 | Insurance policy/certificate amount must cover at least 110% of CIF invoice value; currency must match LC | Invoice CIF USD 100,000; insurance must be ≥ USD 110,000 |
| **Para. K1–K6** | Certificates of Origin | Art. 14d | Country of origin on certificate must not contradict invoice; issuer must be as stipulated in LC | CoO states "Made in China" but invoice states "Made in Germany" = contradiction |

## Notes

1. **Strict vs. Substantial Compliance** — UCP 600 applies a **strict compliance** standard. Even minor deviations that do not affect meaning may be raised as discrepancies by some banks. ISBP 821 moderates this by providing guidance on acceptable variances (abbreviations, minor typos, address formats).

2. **All-or-Nothing Rule (Art. 16)** — If a bank decides to refuse a presentation, it must list **all discrepancies in a single notice**. It cannot come back with additional discrepancies after the first notice.

3. **Waiver by Applicant (Art. 16f)** — The buyer can instruct the issuing bank to waive discrepancies and release payment. This does not retroactively make the invoice compliant — it is a commercial decision.

4. **Automated Checker Scope** — An automated invoice checker should implement, at minimum, checks against: Art. 18a, 18b, 18c, 14c, 14d, 14i + ISBP Para. C1–C10 to cover the most common real-world discrepancies.
